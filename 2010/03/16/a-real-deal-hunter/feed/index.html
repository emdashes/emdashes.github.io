I used to never really save much on anything until I got married! Now, I try to save on sales when I can: Groceries and pharmaceuticals are where we try to take advantage the most. We try to get the best travel deals too, especially when it comes to transportation. That said, we won’t go for any low – the cheapest doesn’t always win. We’re also concerned with how and where something gets produced and we’re willing to pay for that.
There are consequences to always going for cheap – and I don’t just mean that we sometimes get crappy stuff. Those consequences can affect the people who do the work to produce the goods and services we receive. To that end I think that Luke 16:1-14 outlines some excellent principles for how we should view “cheap”.
