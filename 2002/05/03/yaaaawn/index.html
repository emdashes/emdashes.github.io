Yaaaawn!

Posted byEmily May 3, 2002 Leave a comment on Yaaaawn!

Wednesday was quite um adventurous. Firstly, my utmost apologies to Danny and Chris for making them late for prayer meeting. And much thanks for them in helping my cousin(s) move into their new place. Wow, that place is sure nice! I’m looking fwd to live there tomolo :). After the move, Marcy and I got dropped off at St. Laurie’s because Marcy had to go home to do some project work (*groan*) so he couldn’t make it to prayer meeting. We stopped to grab a hearty meal (well not really, but nevertheless…) and we dropped by Sunglass Hut to see how Jonnie was doin’. I ended up trying to persuade Marcy to come w me to SportCheck to see if those shoes were still available but I got distracted by this whole crowd of ppl standin’ front of Baskin Robbins. I wanted to see what the commotion was all about and saw this really cool banner that advertised for a free scoop of ice cream for each customer! YUMMY! (I totally forgot that each year on May 1st, BR gives out a FREE scoop of ice cream nationwide). I got a sherbet and Mumbles ended up w a sugar cone (*envy!*). After, Jon convinced me to eat s’more by persuadin’ us to go w him to East Sides, so basically I ate non-stop … b/c I couldn’t resist the food and so ordered a bruschetta. mmmmm…nummy! Don’t think I’ve had 3 meals w/in such a short time frame. Tho, it was more like appetizer, entrée and dessert in the wrong order.

Thursday was yesterday. After buying those shoes (alone b/c Mumbles wasn’t about to go w me but that served to be betta b/c I was able to buy other things I needed as well), I bused home. Later, as Marcy went to another place for his “project” meeting, I met up w SilQ, Jonnie-boy, and JJ at St. Lauries. Ended up going to Ottawa U to check out job postings but there were none applicable. We made fun of each other all day, which strangely made me warmer as I walked in the cold. Because the boyz were hungry, we stopped at Dunn’s for some grub. Tho’ SilQ almost got lost on his way to Scotiabank. We went all the way to Elgin Street and Frank (Elgin St. Diner) before finding it. I didn’t want to spend anymore unncessary $ on grub so I just kinda watched them eat this yummy smoked meat poutine (tho Jonnie had 2 weiners :P) and drank my grapefruit juice. Went to Rideau Centre afer and they scrounged around for Mumbles belated bday present and kinda killed time. Somehow, we ended up in Chapters and I ended up in the Bargain Books section and I managed to find a Asian cookbook encyclopedia that I liked. It’s rare that I buy any books, much less cookbooks! I flipped through the book and noticed that most of the receipes seemed quite authentic w readily-available ingredients. Has tons of coloured pix of “cutting” and various cookin’ techniques, as well w look-alikes of what seems “Chinese” as well as other Asian dishes, and an index (helps me when I have ingredients but dunno what to make). Even teaches you how to cook rice. HAHA. At first I wasn’t gonna even take a glance at it b/c of an “white” last name of the contributing editor, but upon closer inspected, I read that it was printed and bound in China, specifically HK! Thankfully, SiLQ has a Chapters Club card so I got 10% off ;). So, as much as it was ($14.99), I hope to improve my cooking skills and expand my “menu” by the end of the summer (and I’ll invite y’all over and poison ya *muhahahah*):P. Speaking of food, had dinner at Joey’s Only Seafood in Merrivale to celebrate Mr. Geezer’s belated bday :). Tho’ I think Red Lobster or La Pointe serves better seafood. Tho’ this place is less expensive. On Tuesdays it’s $6.95 for all-you-can-eat fish and chips there, just like what we have over in BC. I had that once there in Abbotsford or Langley b/c my dad knew the owner of that particular restaurant.

Today, Marcy woke me up at 800 hrs and somehow persuaded me to go meet up w him at Carleton U so I could go look for some jobs at the employment centre. Guess what? That boy made me wait for him til like 1 PM! At first was gonna meet me there but by the time I got there, he said he was comin’ at 10:30, so I decided to finish Amy Tan’s book The Bonesetter’s Daughter (thank God I decided to bring a book). I called him at around 11 am and he said he was comin’ and didn’t show til close to 1 when I finished my book (yes, I read fast but that book wasn’t that good. I still like The Kitchen God’s Wife the best) and took a nap. I was sooooo hungry and tired by the time he found me half awake at the library. There’s a reason why anything to do w his “project” isn’t all to positive in my books. I can’t believe Carleton’s library. They forbid cell phones and food and drinks! Wah, so restrictive! How would anyone contact me and what if I’m hungry? I bought this tea and doughnut at Timmy Ho’s figuring that I could snack while I read, but when I finally found my way to the library via the labrinyth of tunnels (no worries, I only made 1 wrong turn), I got there and to my utter dismay they didn’t let me have food! So, anyhow, I plopped down w my unopened tea at hand and my bag of doughnut on the table and shortly, this staff dude comes by and says that I can’t have food. I muttered something to the lines of, “yeah, I’m not gonna open this”, and he’s like, “well, just make sure you conceal it so not to encourage others to bring food in here”. Hmm, what a dandy idea. While I sat for about 3 hrs and as my tea got cold, my stomach began to rumble and so I carefully ended up eating my “concealed” entire honey cruller in the library bite by bite as I was sitting right next to the info desk.

Spent afternoon and evening at my aunt’s place again. Helped the kids in their Chinese tho’ I’m not entirely sure that I’ve helped them much or correctly for that matter. I hope it helped anyhow; tho it was fun :). I seemed to have picked up more in my understanding of French again. Think I might drop by again next week b/c Fabrice has an exam next week and we’re not sure if my uncle will be back or not by then. I’m amazed at what the kids already know tho’! The second oldest one is already in “Grade 7” and the oldest has moved onto Mandarin (Cdn standards; HK = Grade 2 or 3)– many of the words I can’t remember anymore–basically they’re at my reading level! = | Impressive! Since their mother tongue is prolly French. Camille is prolly the best at the pronounciation but I guess it’s b/c she’s the youngest (still retains and practices more Chinese @ home). Okay, more motivation for me to practice my reading skills this summer!

Moving day tomolo! Kinda excited, kinda hmm…mixture of different feelings…esp to a new place w new ppl…
