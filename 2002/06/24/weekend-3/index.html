Weekend

Posted byEmily June 24, 2002 Leave a comment on Weekend

My weekend in “short”. Note: Pix up from trip on webshots. Saturday: Went to NY for one day (yes, from Ottawa). My mom wanted me to gain some driving experience b/c she didn’t want me to get hurt on my job b/c I’d be driving every day, and well, I haven’t driven in close to 4 years now (I think my driving xperience w the Volvo 850 helped me get up to par as well..thanks DD :] ). So my parents offered to pay for a car rental to let me drive. Hehe. I practically jumped at the opportunity to rent a car so that I could go see the famous 1000 Islands and Boldt’s Castle (that’s their official site; excuse their poor web design. i am only referencing it for reference purpose 😛 here’s a better one).

So, I booked my car two weeks in advance. To tell you the truth, I had wanted to go last weekend but it rained and poured so much that I had to resched our trip, which turned out to be interesting. Why? Because I found out that car rental companies have different rates per week. The week I reserved a car online, the quoted rates for the compact and economy sized cars at Thrifty’s was approx. $28/day. So, of course, I chose the largest one in the price range (compact). However, when I tried to postpone my reservation, the website quoted for the following week as being $49! yipes. So what else do I do? I turn on my see-lai thinking cap, and call the company to see if I can keep my quoted price and lo and behold, it worked (I later learned, through the rental agent that they actually have a 4-level tier that fluctuates in the year and b//c peak in the summer)! =)

Thus, yesterday, Marcy and I bused to the airport to pick up my car (woohoo, I had a car to actually call “my own” for the day :P). Turns out that they didn’t have any Chrysler Neons left (blech!) and upgraded me to a specialty car, which was the 2002 Jeep Liberty Limited Edition. I remember that in the old days, rental cars were pretty junkie and often dirty and stunk like stale cigarette smoke. Now, we get NEW cars, along with the “new car smell”–talk about good customer service :). Yes, a “mini” SUV. I would’ve never imagined that one of my first vehicles that I could drive would be a 4×4 SUV–but I’d drive this than the Neon any day!. I think this one is rather “cute”…but kinda tipsy..it’s funny b/c there’s a sticker warning in the vehicle that reminds us of that factor. Not to mention that the 3.7L V6 engine (which we got) boasts of 210 HP and 235 lb-ft torque! The best part is that the seat is soo high up that I can actually see OVER the dashboard w/o having to sit up really straight =). I was soo comfie in the driver’s seat! (Side note: Just rode in a Miata that drifted and turned nicely at high speeds =P) We left in late morning and arrived at around 12:30 PM. We had lunch in the car and then took the boat cruise to the island. It’s a wee lil antique lookn boat that ferried us across the St. Lawrence River to Heart Island for $11/person. Other higher class boats were more expensive at $24/person! I’m kinda glad we saved on that b/c the admission to Boldt’s Castle was $7.10 (cdn: fluctuates with USD $4.25)/person. The captain told us stories of surrounding islands on its way to/from the castle. For example, we saw the house built on the island by the Wrigley (Gum) family, an island owned by a frat (seen The Skulls? Same frat.) from Yale, an island with only a picnic table and dog shack, islands that had pine trees on one side, and maples on the other (due to microclimates), a big island that had only trees growing on it and a big cross erected upright to commemorate the family’s son’s fatal death from falling off a cliff (and forbade to build on island thereonin), and an American and Canadian island that was connected by the “world’s shortest international bridge”. There is no one island that’s half American, half Canadian for the “world’s longest peaceful border” goes around these islands. Ah yeah, they need to check for photo ID and proof of citizenship since it’s in Alexandria Bay, NY if you ever go to the Castle. The castle didn’t really have a moat or a drawbridge (not a visible drawbridge anyhow) but it did have a servants’ underground passage that led to a pool…actually, most of the place has been restored (ie. original structure has detriorated). The bowling alley in the playhouse is in the process of restoration (water leakage)…the wooden floors kinda felt soggy for some reason so it felt like it was gonna give away at anytime…the story behind this island is that a man bought it and built it for his wife (what a guy eh?! that wife must’ve felt special indeed!). Unfortunately, midway, his wife suddenly died and he received a telegram and ceased work immediately. That poor fellow must’ve been heart-broken…I really like the architectural work on it tho :). They also had a Yacht House, which we didn’t go to b/c they charge ya another $4 or so for admission. Finished day early and drove back :). Twas a very fun adventure! =) Sunday: Ribs Fest. Words say all. Mmmm…
