Spendin' $

  •  By  Emily
  • On  Jul 13, 2002
  • In  Life

I think that my field visits to the Market is something I look forward to b/c of the crowds of ppl and the many things to look at..however, it tends to tempt me into buying. oddly, not clothes, but FOOD =). The smells of bread baking and the sounds of preparing various foods and drinks just makes me wanna eat. Tha’s kinda how I ended up munchin’ down a gelato (piccolo grande: was there for a fv), a homemade ice cream (see previous entry), and purchased some interesting mustard (Wasabi lime) for Marcy. I have yet to enjoy a beavertail 🙂

Aiya, my cheapest textbook for next school term is $50. The most xpensive one is $121.95 =(. I hope to find some at Chapter’s so maybe then I can take advantage and purchase the 10% off card 🙂 Speaking of textbooks, I ended up in Place D’Orlèans yesterday to purchase some essentials from Wal-mart and ended up shopping around. Gap seems to be having an incredible sale (25% off of already sale items), and Coles has a final clearance on “As is” books. I ended up purchasing a txtbook called Writing Software Documentation by the same publishers as my previous textbook Designing Visual Language that I bought new at UW for $70.95. The funniest thing is that the Software Documentation one is a literal bounded photocopy of the real book; I brought it to the counter and asked the girl, “Is this a real/legit book?” She said it prolly is but couldn’t really tell me. But anyhow, I bought it anyway b/c I found better condition one for $9.99 (and asked for the $7.99 price b/c there was another one which was in a “worser” shape) ;). Hope this book would be a good reference for my course next term as well as for my future career :).

In the process, I also ended up at Grand and Toy to look through their clearance items as well and found myself buying another Pentel (tho Sanford/Uniball is betta) gel hybrid pen for my note-taking classes next term for $0.50. And an accessory keychain for my bag (retails for $7.99; got it for $1.99); I think it’s cute :D.

Oh! I almost forgot to mention how my work day ended at the Market today. I remembered that Jon has this nasty lookin’ rash from gettin’ henna the other day, so I asked one of the booths (George Street and ByWard Market Sq.) what was in their ingredients. The henna guy was very informative and told me about the risks of chemicals in so-called henna, which produce side-effects including rashes (from chemical burns) and to the extremes, liver infection. If any stand advertise that it would stay for more than 10 days, it’s likely to contain PPD, a chemical that’s used in hair dyes (clue: “black henna”). Here’s an excerpt from an e-mail I sent out to my fellowship which refers one to the henna website.

I have pulled the following off a similar website:

If you have received “black henna” and experience oozing sores, itching, blistering or rashes anytime up to 4 weeks after application, you probably have a PPD chemical burn! Do not try to treat it yourself. This is a serious chemical burn from a toxin, not an allergic reaction, and must be attended to professionally.

If a person has a reaction to traditional henna (just mild itching, not raised oozing blisters and scars), it is usually because of a sensitivity to the clove oil, mehlabiya oil, henna oil, or eucalyptus oil which were added to the mix. A person with sensitive skin should ask for traditional henna with only lemon juice added.
