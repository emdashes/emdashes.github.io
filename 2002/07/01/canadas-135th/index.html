Canada's 135th

Posted byEmily July 1, 2002 Leave a comment on Canada's 135th

UW Bulletin (Friday, June 29, 2002): Willis Au, a second-year civil engineering student, died Sunday evening in Toronto, his home city. Visitation is this Saturday at 10 a.m., followed by the funeral at 11 a.m., at the Toronto Chinese Mennonite Church, 1038 Woodbine Avenue (corner of Danforth). I feel that God’s name was indeed lifted up and proclaimed that day after reading Pork’s Sunday, June 30th entry. And from the sounds of it, the funeral and burial service went as well as it could’ve for such an occasion. Saturday: Spent afternoon at the Mud Oven w Marcy. I d/n realise that figuring out a design an then painting it would be harder than I thought it is…evenin’ w Josh and Marc for Starbucks Fraps and dinner (tho J d/n eat) at Shawarma King on Bank St. City-wide P&W @ the Met (better this time b/c corporate prayer instead of groups), and more food w Hilman and Jon at Shawarma King yet again. Sunday: helped Jon move into his new AC place and had dinner there. So tiring. Monday: Canada Day. No wonder my feet really really hurt today at work. I was walking all day and was up at 7:32 AM. Marc called to wake me up and I ended up spending half of my day w Marc and his family downtown. I realised that it was my first time w his family doing smthing outside of the house. Actually, we ended up eating at 2 of the places that Marc told me to try, one of which was Eggspectation (eggstravagent, eggpensive, and eggcellent food =P). Walked to Parliament Hill to see horse dung everywhere and ppl stepping IN them. Actually, that was more of a result from the RCMP parade/musical ride thingy. Poor Simon, he was stuck in 43C (w humidex) weather as a guard for the changing of the guard ceremony. Saw some red ‘chuters jump from planes. Afterwards, we decided to venture into the NAC to cool off from the heat and watched this yawn dance performance by the NAC School of Dance. Normally, I enjoy watching ballets and contemporary dances (drimes, hip hop, etc.), but I donno whether this collection was supposed to show off the talent of its students or not? In btwn, we caught a glimpse of the snowbirds flying in formation. Subway for lunch. I’m starting to get sick of these subs, but at least they still have enuff hot subs and bread/toppings/dressings to choose from and they sure taste betta than McD’s :). Headed twd Major Hill’s park in hopes for me to get the new Cdn quarter, which is in circulation til September 2002, but the line was insanely long and it was insanely hot, so we ended up at the National Arts Gallery to cool down. We ventured into the Garden Court area and bumped into J and Ange. So, we just vegged there for hrs upon end while we waited for ppl to show: napping, talking, goofin’ off, and playin’ cards. Frankly, I’m surprised that security didn’t kick us out. Adrian and Chris, Cyn, Ben chan finally decided to arrive so we headed to Imperial Palace (that’s the second place to eat as recommended by Marcy–aside from Eggspectation). The Chinese food there is pretty good =). Unsurprisingly, the Chinese menu has a lot more “good” choices than the English one. Thankfully, I was able to read some of the dishes and some of us ended up trying “Toong Choy” (aka oong choy). Yay! Oong choy is in season =)–I’ll remember that next time I go to MK :P. Firewrks started at 10:05 pm and we all crwded on Major Hill’s Park. The rest of the other guys (Dom, J, Ange, Tim Poon, Deb, Scott Yee, Andy, Matt, Hilman, Dawn, etc.) were at Parliment Hill. While waiting, Adrian and I walked around and we ended up seeing these 3 guys making music (imitiation all types of sound) all w their vocal chords. Neat eh? :). Ange later told me that they missed 25% of it b’c of the buildings, so I’m rather glad that we (Chris, Ben Chan, Marc, Jon, Bert, Adrian, Cyn, and later Elaina and Ben Hu, didn’t take Dawn’s advice to join them at the Hill. Took advantage of my slower shutter speed on my dig cam and took some pix. Unfortunately, my hands naturally shake, so mine didn’t turn out too well, but the couple that Marcy took on my cam were okay :). All in all, the fireworks were definitely worth it! Yaay! Tho’ I missed them last year, I got to see them this year. My cos claims that they heard the resonance of the big ones even in Gloucester-Ottawa area! One observation tho: July 1 seems to be a very odd day: Yesterday was super hot (43C w humidex: hottest of entire summer thus far aside from today), and last year was the coldest (6C). I neglected to mention the three brats that kicked my bag around and got it dirty. Basically, we were in an area of space and our bags were on the floor (decorated w sidewalk chalk in front of us by the fence so basically our bodies surrounded the bags). As the fireworks were about to start, 3 kids (7-11 year olds?) pushed their way in and stood in front of us. Elaina and I gave each other looks and kinda said, “what the heck?!” in Chinese. I was gonna shrug it off but then they started to kick our bags around. Cyn askd them to be careful of our stuff, but they still walked all over our stuff. I wasn’t too pleased and started talking behind their backs in Chinese. Elaina, tho, bascially said everything in English, asking to watch where they stepped. The oldest daughter proceeded to talk back and say, “well, your bags shouldn’t be on the floor to begin with. You should watch where you put your things”. If I’d known who their parents were, I would’ve gave the parents a piece of my mind. First, discipline your kids. Secondly, teach them some manners. They talk back, are rude, and very inconsiderate. Not only did they not say, “Excuse me” (cept when they tried to go out and we didn’t really let them) or apologise, as the boys left their spots, the girl tried to hog her body across the “empty” space. Anyhow, I felt that last year’s Veggie Tales keeners were a lot more tolerable than these kids. Elaina ended up saying that she was tempted to not have kids because of these people. heheh. I persuaded her to teach her kids well if she ever ends up having them ;P.]]>
