Babywearing

Posted byEmily June 17, 2017 Leave a comment on Babywearing

controversial warning to watch out for “crotch-danglers” (ie. instead of dangling by their legs, that they’re supported by a seat or their legs are in a M / froggy style so that they’re not contributing to hip dysplasia), so we returned it, since she didn’t seem to take to it anyway and because I didn’t want to risk making her hip-clicks any worse. Thankfully, that wasn’t the end of babywearing: a $21 hybrid mei dai / stretchy wrap by MamanKangourou seemed to be a good find which ended up working really well for us when she was smaller[ref]minor complaint are the long straps[/ref] – at that point, I had no idea what the carries were (mei dai / stretchy wrap?) and had never heard of the company, but I enjoyed its coziness / snugglies, the extra neck support, and she enjoyed napping in it especially during Sunday church service, and when she was big enough to fit in a suspected fake[ref]which the company refused to confirm because it wasn’t purchased from the retailer vs. Sophie the Giraffe’s company who confirmed that a hand-me-down one was old, but authentic[/ref] ergo hand-me-down without the infant insert, she liked to be carried in that as well – even into toddlerhood. A friend allow loaned us a linen Sakura Bloom ring sling, but I didn’t have the heart to use it all that much because of its cost! Baby #2 might have been neglected in some things for better or worse (#secondbaby), but this time around, I looked into getting a ring sling that wasn’t crazy expensive and found one on Etsy for $40 after not being able to source any local handmade ones. When it came, it wasn’t as soft as I expected so it was harder to adjust, but with the help of my chiropractor, she showed me how to use it so that it’s comfortable for baby or toddler and me; maybe it wasn’t broken in yet, but now I know that the sling is probably made of duck cloth (one-sided print). Enter the DIY Babywearing Facebook group. I don’t know how I ended up on there, but I’ve learned a lot from this community, with one successfully completed project: an Ikea Stilfull tablecloth converted to two soft no-sew (but hemmed) ring slings! Had I known about how easy and inexpensive it is to make your own wrap/carrier, I would have done it from the start, and used $15 instead of paying $40 for one. I’m likely at a base size 5 so I can use the Stilfull tablecloth length for size 2 carries and under when I don’t want to use the rings, including the following front/hip carries: Source: http://wiki.babywearingdiy.com/do/view/Main/WrapCarries

Half Front Wrap Cross Carry HFWCC Base – 3 Beginner friendly
Semi Front Wrap Cross Carry Semi-FWCC Base – 3 Good First Carry
Semi Front Wrap Cross Carry with a Poppins Finish Base – 3
Kangaroo Base – 3
Poppins Hip Carry Base – 3
No No No Hip Carry Base – 4
Traditional Sling Carry (FKA Rebozo Carry) TSC Base – 5 Good First Carry
No-Sew Ring Sling Base – 5 Beginner friendly
Someday, I might attempt the back carries, maybe practicing with the older one first?
Ruck Tied Under Bum RTUB Base – 4 none 1 back pass
Ruck Tied at Shoulder RTAS Base – 4 Optional chest belt 1 back pass
Short Back Cross Carry (NOT leanproof, only appropriate for trustworthy toddlers and experienced wrappers. Click here for more info.) SBCC Base – 4 Optional chest belt 2 back passes
Strap Carry Base – 4 varies varies
Traditional Back Carry (FKA Rebozo Back Carry) TBC Base – 5 Chest cross pass 1 back pass
By the way, I got my weight-tested rings from babywearings.ca, a partnered Canadian reseller of slingrings.com, which is pretty much the go-to place to get babywearing rings. They’re good because of their weight and because they aren’t welded together so they won’t risk tearing the fabric or irritating the baby – my baby used to like gnawing at the rings or sleeping against them. Currently, they have a 40% off sale too (Code: NovaScotia), so it could be anywhere from $4.2 – $6 for a pair! I found the large rings made the Stilfull fabric easier to adjust, but also less grippy, so a pair of medium rings would probably work better once you’re used to how it feels.]]>
