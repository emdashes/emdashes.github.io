Orlando with young kids

Posted byEmily December 23, 2017 Leave a comment on Orlando with young kids

Flights [caption id="attachment_6088" align="alignright" width="225"] This was a creation from her imagination, after spending a day at Legoland![/caption] Flying from and to a small(er) airports (IAG, SFB, PIE) in the American side meant less wait at security and check-in, car-rental pickup and drop-off points[ref] – the car rental pick-up in Sanford was empty as well, which meant no waiting and a lot of vehicles to choose from – so we got to use a fancier Camry SE, not your typical granny Camrys of the past[/ref], and at customs. We even had the luxury of stopping at Wegmans and Trader Joe’s to stock up on goods without weighing down our luggage because had our car, which ended up being parked at a nearby hotel (which was jogging / walking distance). The Best Western at Niagara Falls, NY was only $80 for a night’s stay, free parking while we were away, and breakfast!

Activities

  • Crayola Experience: our kids (and I did too) had a blast checking out the different activity stations and playing on the indoor play structure. The mall that it’s attached to is quite nice too, so you can get some shopping in, or have your kids play in the free play area inside the mall too. Just please make sure they wash their hands properly before eating [ref] Parent fail here as we just gave them hand sanitizer and our oldest somehow managed to get a stomach flu within that day – poor car seat. She was still feeling unwell the next day or so, so much so that she asked to fly home and not stay in the caboose a couple of times the following day[/ref].
    • Keep an eye out for current offers: If you’re staying in the Orlando for an extended time and the weather forecast isn’t great, you can consider buying an annual pass, as it’s only a few dollars more than the general admission. Other offers include free admission within 7 days of your birthday, a 4-person annual pass for $100 or 3 for $80 (valid for 13 months if you buy before the end of this year), 50% off after 5pm, and free admission for teachers with 50% off for their immediate families. We didn’t fit the criteria for either of those, so we found the least expensive option from Costco.com at $16.99 for anyone 3 years old or older.
  • Watch a rocket launch, if it’s during the time you’re there. A rocket launch was scheduled to happen during our week there but it got postponed twice! You can also watch it at a distance for free (especially if your young child isn’t going to find going to the Kennedy Space Center interesting)

Beyond Orlando

  • Beaches:
    • Cocoa Beach, where Kennedy Space Center is located a little over an hour away from the Disney / Orlando area, and has a sandy beach front with a pier that you can walk along (have not visited as the rocket launch we had planned on seeing was postponed)
    • Clearwater Beach and St. Pete’s Beach, both in the same area, a little over an hour away from Disney / Orlando area the other direction is ranked #3 and #4 respectively as the top beaches in the US on TripAdvisor. I was a little disappointed that we missed seeing either of them, but our littlest was sick that day and the temperature and dropped down to a chilly 12C; we spent more time in our caboose and went to a nearby playground instead, which the kids had an equally good time
    • If you don’t want to go as far but still want to play in the water and your resort hotel doesn’t have a kid-friendly pool, you can check out nearby splash pads (free or nominal charge)

Eats

Finding good eats seemed easier in St. Petersburg than in Orlando, but maybe I just didn’t know where to look, but we did have a few favourites:
  • Sweet Tomatoes: soup and salad buffet bar with finger-friendly food ($12 adults, much less for kids, and family pricing that comes with a drink). The salad area, soups, and bread areas had a lot of vegan (thus dairy-free) options and the items that had nuts or seeds in it were labelled; there was even vegan butter (tasted great)! When I had a question, there was also a manager that could check their books or boxes for an ingredient list so I felt safe eating there. The kids ate so much, and the adults liked it so much that we ended up visiting twice. You might even be able to find a coupon to use in-store online.
  • Q’Kenans – Venezuelan cuisine, #6 of almost 3000 restaurants in the Orlando area on TripAdvisor: casual, family-friendly dining space, and the (owner? manager?) was great at answering questions. We came on a friend’s recommendation, which I’m glad for as it’s not a place that I would have gone into based on what it looks like from the parking lot (probably because it reminds me of the soup and sandwich restaurants we had). By the end of our meal, we had a plate of lightly batter calamari with their house sauce and I had an empanada and arepa while j.w chose some homemade cheese dish with a rice-based drink similar to horchata and it only came to $32. I might have tried their patacon plantain sandwich if I hadn’t had one for lunch at a Cuban place days before. Had I known that they have a promo on Groupon, I may have purchased from there first.
  • World’s largest McDonald’s: the menu has some unusual items like pizza, pasta, belgium waffles, croque monsieur, and cheesecake. I didn’t try much there as they couldn’t tell me much about the ingredients in the items that were not listed online (because it was a unique McDonald’s). Perfect for kids to expend their energy on a cooler or wet day, as there is free two-story play structure and an area with priced arcade and midway games. The under 3 play area is very small though, and I would say if you have a mobile toddler, they would get bored and try to play in the main play structure which is supposedly for 4-12 years of age.
  • Other notes:
    • Sanaa at Disney’s Animal Kingdom is on the higher end (Disney pricing, I suppose) and the food only okay, but you’re essentially paying to see animals walk up to the windows and see African safari type animals in the viewing area. Free parking for diners.
    • If you’re at Disney Springs (which sells unique Disney wear at Uniqlo), the Polite Pig also offers some good southern BBQ flavour at expected Disney prices, and can answer my allergy-geared questions – the sides (when you order 3) are the same prices as the sides we’re accustomed to paying; I really enjoyed the BBQ cauliflower and crispy brussel sprouts glazed in whiskey-caramel (though they may have subbed the caramel for me), and the mac&cheese side was a portion big enough for our oldest as a main.

Beyond Orlando

  • Crispy Cajun in Clearwater: the $10ish jambalaya, one of the three top sellers, was delicious and was excellent comfort food for the cold weather we had that weekend and worth its portion in onions – quite spicy even on mild; it was large enough to feed two. We wanted to go back the next day to try their most-popular sale, fried chicken (over a thousand pounds sold in a week), but they were closed on Sundays, sadly. The lady told me that nothing is fried with milk in it (intentionally as she is aware that so much batter is often buttermilk based or some other milk ingredient) so I was one happy eater. She also offered to make some grilled Cajun fish (I changed it from 2 piece to 1 because of her poor appetite due to her stomach flu) served with mashed potatoes (they found the gravy peppery) and only charged us $3! I would’ve also been interested in trying the fried gizzards and hush puppies (3 for $1.50)
  • Big Island Poke in Tampa: it’s just a drive-through place – kind of like the coffee drive-thru places I’ve seen in other cities, but it serves poke bowls, acai bowls, and smoothies, *almost* just like they do in Hawaii. Their fish pieces are decent in size and the toppings were quite fresh, with reasonable pricing; I topped up my meal with a poke bowl because I wasn’t full from Ray’s. I would choose a make-your-own poke bowl next time instead of a suggested one.
  • Big Ray’s Fish Camp in Tampa: has fresh-tasting fish (like grouper) and interesting dessert choices like fried key-lime pie, conch fritters, and grouper cheeks. However, I couldn’t try much of their menu as they use milk ingredients/buttermilk in their batter (which also appears in their fryers) so I was limited to a grilled grouper over some greens.
