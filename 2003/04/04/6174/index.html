Kumosa and My Sister Earlier on in the night, Dave Tse said that he wanted to make sure to honour his fellow brother in Christ in taking care of a Christian sister. Somehow, that led me to a dark, empty, and dusty home, where there was no apparent exit…until one last door we tried and indeed, that was the exit. In another scene, many of us were sitting in an auditorium-like place. Some large male decided to pick on certain individuals (scam or was of some threat)–my sister being one of them. My sister decided to slip quietly out of the room (from the rear exit since she was sitting a row behind me near the door at the upper rear) to alert security. Somehow, the guy seemed to have seen her, so he walked back up the stairs (he was at the front then) and peer out from the exit, with the intention of going after her. I guess the time that was lapsed allowed her a head start. Yet, I think he changed his mind shortly after stepping out the exit and decided to exit by the front doors and hightail outta there. ‘Cept by the time he did that, he encountered two security guards/policemen. Because the two men didn’t let him through, he decided to pull out his gun and fire shots at them (while the whole auditorium was watching mind you). Luckily, they had bullet proof vest and outfits on, so the bullets merely bounced off their chests. Insanely enough and out of pure desperation, the man fired the gun into the crowd, looking for people to hit–including my sister. But her seat was still empty. I think she didn’t dare to come back in. I instinctly ducked behind the seats to shield myself from being a direct target and from any stray shots. Shortly after, I was back exploring a house again, except this time, I was at my aunt’s new home. It was quite a big house, with 3 bedrooms downstairs, the main floor, and 1 master bedroom with a TV room upstairs.  I ended up looking out her window and saw that there were double-decker buses: green and yellow that looked it they were from HK, but she told me that they transported people to and from the harbour. I tried to get a picture of it from my camera, but by the time I set up my shot, they all disappeared. I remember being in a rush to get to the dock so to catch the ferry. I knew the person in the other car/motorcycle who was also lined up for the ferry. The drive there (or from the ferry) was strange, with many windy roads and tight corners. I didn’t do very many shoulder checks at all. I ended up with 3 other people in a place where they lie you down and transport you to another place while you’re unconscious. After the process, you could win something. The participant ahead of us won Kumosa but when the guy in charge read it, he wore a surprised, yet unpleased expression on his face. So, he just said outloud, she won Mosa. I ventured to guess money? And he nodded with the indication that Kumosa meant lots of money. So, the gal won an jacket made of money (sorta like a money tree). Some of us got transported to an island instead, far far away from everyone and everything. I thought that was weird, so I risked it and took the next wave and surfed it down to the shore of the original place. The guy who stood there keeping watch asked me in shock how I got there and what I was doing there. He told me that I would get in trouble if his boss found out and before either of us did, he tried to send me back with something like a canon/catapult. ‘Cept I flew to the wrong co-ordinates and landed elsewhere…]]>
