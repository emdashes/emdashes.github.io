stories over coffee

Posted byEmily December 19, 2003 2 Comments on

A few months ago, an l’il thought popped into my head: go check the Focus on the Family website to look for job postings. I was excited when I got interviewed, because I thought perhaps that l’il push from nowhere was a divine sign. A few weeks after, jaw told me to check out the RIM postings, because there was plenty of new openings. Well, I had my second chance with them, but they called me two days later to tell me that someone internally was promoted to that position. Right before my last interview with RIM, the Vice President of a company called asking me if I was still interested in their company, and for some vague position that had to do with Marketing writing. I said yes, and he told me that some lady would call to arrange an interview with me. I said okay and dismissed it to the back of my mind since I was focused on my other job applications. The weekend transpired and on Tuesday, I received a call from Safe Software (safe.com) asking me to come in for an interview on the following day. I accepted and wasn’t sure at all what I was going to be interviewed for. See, I had not applied for any position with their company, but instead had submitted a generic résumé to my Alpha course group leader at the end of September just because he offered to pass it in for me. He promised nothing, and in turn, I didn’t expect anything either. You know the saying, “Out of sight, out of mind”? Well, it worked. So, after receiving a big zero with the Ministry of Labour’s question, “What do you know about us?”, I did my standard research on the company.

From their website, I found out that they had a 40 employee base, a 4000+ user base, and it was their 10th anniversary this year. Safe also ranked as one of the top 100 technology companies in BC, the 37th fastest growing company in BC, and was a finalist for this year’s Canadian E-Business Leadership award. Quite impressive, eh? The interview was nothing like any formal interview I’ve ever experienced. After telling me about the new position they had, I only recall them asking me two questions: a) What are your strengths and weaknesses? and b) How do you work? (ie. in a quiet environment or the opposite?). Very casual and relaxed – I felt that it was at an equilibrium in terms of how much each side spoke. But then, I wasn’t sure if it was a good sign or not when they didn’t ask me very many questions. And then, they even brought in the VP to meet me! He’s a humorous guy; joked about having me start on Dec. 25 since there would be plenty of office space then. Anyhow, after close to an hour of interviewing and touring the office (w00t! it has a fooseball table), I walked out with a company poster, a newsletter, and a business card.

They told me that they would decide within 1 day, and would notify me by the end of the week. Within two hours of arriving home, I heard from them again. I picked up the phone bracing myself and wondering why they were calling me that soon. They had decided. Although some may say that small companies have their drawbacks, I definitely see a lot of potential for this one. It’s a l’il bigger than Media-X, and because I’ve worked with small(er) companies before, I can tell you that there are benefits of working in a company that size. The Safe Software team seems to be very energetic, enthusiastic, and excited about the people and what they do. I’m definitely looking forward to working with them! I’ll be starting in January, and will begin my career as a Marketing Copywriter. I may stumble and fall on the way, but He’ll be there to pick me up :).
