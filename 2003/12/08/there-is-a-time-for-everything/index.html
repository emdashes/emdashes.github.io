There is a time for everything…

Posted byEmily December 8, 2003 Leave a comment on There is a time for everything…

…and a season for every activity under heaven (Ecclesiastes 3:1). My goodness, what a jammed packed Saturday – including an evening of “firsts”. In the morning, I rolled up my sleeves and received a flu-shot. Thankfully, there were no aftermath effects aside from the mild numbness in my arm. Went w my parents to watch Asian Heros, a Cantonese film (w Engl subtitles) about 5 characters, whose lives are set in a hospital in HK during SARS (based on true stories). I have a nagging suspicion that the movie was not filmed in HK, as led to believe. Early in the movie, there was a shot of “AM Gardens” (representing Amoy Gardens, the apartment complex in HK that was isolated last year due to SARS) but in the corner, part of a sign read 911 (as opposed to 999 since the emergency number in HK, to my knowledge, is still 999).

The evening was spent as a guest with P and V at “A Very Vegas Christmas Party”. I had the privilege to join in the festivities of EA’s company party. If “company Christmas party” ever had any negative connotations to it (ie. boring, slow, etc.), this one was far from those adjectives. With most of the attendees under 35 years of old, it seemed more like a big high-school after grad event than a company party. And, Vegas indeed! The Commodore Ballroom was transformed into mini-Vegas, with golden pillared “palm trees”, casino tables with professional dealers and play money, food galore (eg. sushi, roast ham, cheesecake, etc.), free drinks (2 each, though a reliable source says that Mainframe’s (Reboot) party this weekend got 4), and not to mention showgirls and other forms of live entertainment, along with excellent choice of dance music (ranging from Abba’s “Dancing Queen” to Alice Deejay’s “Better Off Alone”). Got to see some sneak previews of some awesome games that are out this year and the next (Shame that I don’t have a console; I wanna play SSX3!).

Echoing V’s thoughts, when asked if we wanted something to drink. — Drink? What is there to drink? I’ve never ordered a drink before consulting a drink menu/bar list. — Anyhow, after thinking the drinks I’ve drank before and knew about, I recalled a citrus juice with grenadine and vaguely remembered it as Tequila Sunrise. I only used 1 of my 2 vouchers because I didn’t want it to affect my driving in any way after the party.

After winning/losing some at the gambling wheel, we made our way to a Blackjack table and P managed to squeeze into a spot. Talking without shouting above the music and din, was next to impossible. Walking without being squished was also next to impossible. However, I was introduced to a few co-workers that P and V knew – the most impressionable (likely because I spoke with them the most) were D and J. D knew P since high school and J was my classmate’s older brother! I would’ve never guessed – they seem to be complete opposites. After leaving for a bit, J came back to see if I wanted to dance because P was still playing, but I was just starting to play (=p) so I convinced him to play with us for a few rounds before he left us to the game to dance again.

Thankfully, I was playing Blackjack because I don’t dance nor do I enjoy making a fool outta myself (maybe another drink would’ve done the trick), by myself, in front of a stranger. In any event, after getting bored of Blackjack, P and I couldn’t persuade V to the dance floor, so I took a risk and just joined in for the very first time (yes, i have not been to fed hall in my 5 years at uw apart for grad pics and career fair). I was more of an observer on the dance floor: some were needlessly drunk or definitely buzzed in their crazed dancing, others were sorta swaying to the beat – either dragged on or accompanying their significant others/friends, and still others that definitely had style. There was one chick who had the whole sensual “look-at-my-bod” dance going on the stage, and this other stylin’ chick who really danced with the song (a good version of britney). Looking around, I don’t think DDR or para-para really works in settings like these =þ.

Admittedly, after arriving home past 1 am and not going to bed right away, I was really tired the next day, and it hit during Sunday School. Isaac noted that although I zoned out,it seemed that whenever the prof went to the next point, I’d mysteriously open my eyes and jot it down. Thus, he wasn’t sure if I was sleeping or just picturing the images as Uncle Gordon said he does by closing his eyes during the sermons…er, yeah…
