Windows of the Soul

Posted byEmily January 28, 2003 Leave a comment on Windows of the Soul

A psychological and spiritual look into the dreams of one’s self–PT lent me this book as a result of our previous discussion on dreaming and sin. Meier and Wise seem to discredit more of Freud’s theories and instead, support arguments from Carl Jung in regards to dreams and symbols. Like PT, I’m not entire sure I agree with the ideas of the books or the encouragement for one to do dream-works. The book encourages one to remember his/her dreams and to analyze them into full detail. I don’t seem to have problems doing that, but sometimes I would like to not remember my dreams and be at peace. For one, the authors seem to indicate dreaming of a having a sensual relationship with another of the opposite sex is not abnormal, or is it anything to worry bout (ie. being wrong) for they write, “The next time your mate talks aloud to another person in his or her dreams, don’t get suspious or worried. To the contrary, the spouse is probably defusing the problem of stray attractions!” (191). Hrm, I’m not sure how that works, but okay if that’s how they get comforted…I wonder if that also works for non-married couples…On the other hand, the authors note that predictive dreams (dreams that in hindsight seem to indicate reality) does “not cause us to anticipate future as much as to recognise the work of God when it comes to pass”. I believe that statement makes sense for when I dreamt about “finding” my friend Tommy, I didn’t realise that it would come true and when it did, I did remember my dreams…and my actions/prayers to God regarding them. Maybe I’ll check out a similar book in the future entitled, Dreams and Visions by Jane Hamon to see what she hasta say about dreams in another spiritual/biblical perspective.

Speaking of dreams, at 7:58 am this morning, I was abruptly shaken from my dreams by a *CRASH! SLAM! BUMP!*, followed by an “AH! I can’t see! Why is everything so dark?!” and then a millisecond of silence and then a painful groan.

With that, I instinctly knew that there was something wrong. I recalled that temporary blindness happens if and when there is pressure on the brain against the optical nerve (or something like that) which then causes one to not be able to see. I jumped outta bed, yanked my door open, and peered into the hall with my mind still in a haze and muttered, “Is everything alright?!” and was met with my housemate’s bf coming into the door heading towards her room. At the instance that I saw him, I knew that he’d be able to take care of anything so I was reassured and headed back to bed, tho’ with much worry. Within seconds, I heard, “aarg, I feel like puking!”. Thus, I hustled a prayer towards the heavens praying for her safety, comfort, and healing and relunctantly went back to a restless sleep.

I suspected that she was taken to the hospital especially when the Yellow Pages was left open on the dinner table to the Taxi/Cabs page. Thankfully, she’s okay now, resting in her bed. She told me today that she bumped her head against a hard surface two days ago but did not feel any effects until yesterday, when she was slightly naseous, which also resided. However, this morning, when she was brushing her teeth and putting her contacts on, her vision suddenly blacked-out and she must’ve said everything I’ve heard (but she couldn’t remember) before passing out on the floor in the bathroom. When I opened my door, in my state of confusion, I did not notice her on the bathroom floor for all I saw was her boyfriend rushing into the house. Because I did not know about her previous fall or that she was suffering from post-concussion syndrome (as the doctor identified her to have today), I would’ve likely called 911 as a response to her phrase of not being able to see (fainting I can deal with for I’ve done that myself twice in a day at BAC) but thankfully, her bf came just in time (God’s timing!). The taxi was not to the hospital but rather to the Health Clinic, where after examination, they sent her to ER on a stretcher in an ambulance! There, they proceeded to do a CAT scan, draw 5 vials(?)/bags of blood, and placed her on IV for a few hours. Nothing seemed to be out of order, so they sent her home (where she proceeds to attend class b/c of attendance marks!) and so here she rests. Thank God that she’s okay, for my mind was quite unsettled during my classes today.

Aside: The wallpaper on my desktop needs to be changed. Although it’s a lovely scenery shot of BC (by Boy?), and I see can “touch” the mountains and the waters, everytime I gaze at the waters for too long, my bladder decides to shrink some..hahaha…purely psychological I tell ya!
