Jetsgo? Jetslo!

  •  By  Emily
  • On  Jan 6, 2003
  • In  Life
  • Facebook
  • Twitter

First off, a happy new year to everyone I have yet to seen/greet!
Hope your holidays were as relaxed as mine. Got to spend time with my
family/relatives (well, not really my sis/bro b/c they were either always
out or at WC), and in particular some/many days doing nothing at home
or mainly doing “nothing” w Boy
and ma other friends (HS friends, Cal et co., Chris the Chef, MurE, and
Tommy (whom I haven’t seen for about 2 years or so)…tho I missed seeing
LB).

This section is Part II of my first rant…after being delayed for
another 1.5+ hours (from 23h05 to 12h45 quoted departure time, but by
then we didn’t even board yet), the flight was relatively smooth as
I was exhausted from boarding that morning/afternoon (details to come
in next section) so I just slept. The sunrise during the 4+ hr flight
over the praries (sp?) was beautiful–the three hues of blue, orange,
and pink…Shame that my digcam wasn’t available. ARGH. The most frustrating
part of this flight wasn’t the delayed arrivals or departures, which
they blamed on the snowstorm in TO (I don’t really believe them as Air
Canada didn’t seem to have any problems throughout the day), but rather
the chaotic MESS they made of the retrieval of our baggages/luggages.
We arrived around 8h45(?) but didn’t actually get out of the airport
til way past 10h00! Allegedly, the plane was too heavy and couldn’t
travel properly in the snowstorm, so they had to leave behind some baggage
from random passengers. Unfortunately for us, one of us was the victim
and 2 of her 3 things were left bhind. After waiting in line to be served
by 1 person and filling out some forms, we learnt that the baggages
wouldn’t arrive til later in the afternoon by courier, which meant that
our travel plans to Niagra Falls would’ve been canned. Luckily, Eunie’s
parents were willing to stay home to wait for the package.

And of course, knowing Jetsgo, during that afternoon the bag/snowboard
didn’t make it. Eunie, Becks, Mel, and I trekked around Niagra Falls/Clifton
Hills area (from top of Ripley’s Believe it or not Museum to parking
took us 1 hr walk) and took some pix (w my APS), which hopefully turns
out okay :). The exercise was really nice because Mel and I needed to
work off our stiff bodies (from head to toe and Boy, I can just see
you laffin!). The shower at night really helped b/c I managed to stretch
some of it off =P. Jetsgo apparently called Eunie’s house the next morn
and her parents had to be late for church b/c Beck’s stuff came at 10am.
Thankfully, this time the stuff arrived in one piece. Tho’ if I was
Becky, I would write the company a complaint letter because of the inconvenience
caused, as well as the trouble of having to get a new toothbrush/socks/etc.

And now, the reason for my pain: snowboarding! Yes, what fun!
Mel and I went snowboarding (w/o prior experience in skiing either)
for the very first time on Friday, just hours before our flight back
to Ontario. Yes, we are quite “sad”– haven’t done stuff that tourists
pay tons for to do out West. My dad suggested that I go on New Year’s
but because my bro refused to teach Mel and I, I wasn’t quite psyched
up or had any gear ready to go when Boy offered on New Year’s eve to
take/teach us the day after. However, after a miscalculation of $97
to $84, I decided that even tho’ I could likely rent and get a discount
lift pass elsewhere another time, Mel and I decided to go on the 5th
to Cypress.
Mou (or Becks, but somehow, I’m useta seeing her name as Mou now :P)
kindly lent me her gear (gloves, pants, touque) and my sis lent me her
pants and my bro, his old snowboarding jacket. For some strange reason
tho, my butt got all wet even w 2 track/water resistant pants on, and
Mel’s stayed perfectly dry…ah wellz :).

Contrary to a friend’s slightly-biased opinion, Boy is actually quite
a good instructor and after many, many falls, we got the basics by the
end of the day (went from about 9 am til 3 pm w a lunch break in-btwn)
:). However, it took us soo long to get down the runs and thus, we only
had time to go down two: EasyRider (the bunny hill three times?) and
Collins (green once: took us over an hour to accomplish…I especially
hated the ICE at the top of that run…poor Boy, he had to sit and wait
for us to get up from our many wipe outs). By the end of the day tho’,
I was about to have some control by talking to my snowboard and pointing
in the direction I wanted to go, and most of all, I ended the run off
upright and at a full stop 🙂 (I still can’t ride off the lifts properly
tho). Hahah, I ended up leaving my license and my credit card in my
bro’s jacket so when I was at the the casino in Niagra (no gambling),
I had to show my passport for ID purposes :P. Snowboarding again? Yes!

And that ends my Christmas holiday. Another term begins, and hopefully
by the end of this one, I’ll be done!
