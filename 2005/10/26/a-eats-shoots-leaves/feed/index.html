]]> By: 001 https://emdashes.github.io/2005/10/26/a-eats-shoots-leaves/#comment-277 Wed, 26 Oct 2005 23:55:58 +0000 http://480#comment-277 In majority of the cases, the placement of punctuation is self-evident. Only in very rare cases do they make substantial differences. And even so, a full-blown doctrine does not rely on one single verse.
As for the reference to thief and paradise, neither of the variations contribute to the Catholic idea of purgatory.
Even Protestants have different ideas about our “intermediate state” (the state between our deaths and our resurrection).
My view is that “paradise” here should not be equated with the common conception of “heaven”. It is rather referring to our intermediate state.
And by the way, you will never find direct scriptural references about us “going to heaven” after we die.
To use strictly biblical terms, upon our deaths, we await our future resurrection. That will happen at the Lord’s coming. The Bible speaks of the new city, New Jerusalem, coming down “from heaven to EARTH,” and we shall be in the “new heaven and new EARTH.”
In other words, the eternal kingdom that Jesus is going to establish is actually on earth, not heaven! The second coming (or “parousia”) is actually the time when finally heaven meets earth. Our conventional use of the term “heaven” is not strictly biblical (“After I die, I go to heaven”).
