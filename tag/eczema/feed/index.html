  • Herba Portulacae (1.8g extracted from 15g): targets liver, large intestine
  • Fructus Kochiae (0.9g extracted from 10g): targets kidney, bladder; alleviates itching
  • Radix Sophorae Flavescentis (0.6g extracted from 6g): targets liver, intestine; alleviates itching
  • Radix Et Rhizoma Rhei (0.6g extracted from 3g): targets large intestine; also known as rhubarb!
  • Pericarpium Zanthoxyli (0.6g extracted from 6g): targets stomach; relieves itching (perhaps because of the numbing properties? the Chinese name “hua jiao” reminded me of this tongue numbing prickly heat sauce that my parents have in their cupboards, which I often associate with sichuan peppers)
  • Folium Isatidis (0.9g of 10g): targets liver and stomach

Especially not because of the cost and because we are already seeing a dermatologist (and thus East meets or conflicts with West?) and because our first outgrew most of hers. Admittedly, with only one treatment so far, it seems to be more effective in speedy results compared with the continued steroid / cortisone use (which I don’t want to rely on anyway); I just don’t know if there are going to be side effects or if the problem will come back and I wasn’t told what the products would do so it feels like she expects me to trust her in blind faith. I know baby skin and bodies are just so much more sensitive than our adult ones, and because it absorbs everything like a sponge and their little systems are still growing, I still have reservations on whether we made the right choice – maybe it’s an answered prayer since it coincided around the same time. So maybe the $220+ will be worthwhile ($100 for consultation fee, $50 for the packets and $70 cream…yipes), but maybe not.
