Lesson #21453…and counting

  • Try to bring someone with you who has bought a new vehicle before when you go to a dealership and negotiate for a vehicle (ideally, my parents)
  • Bring a calculator with you
  • Make sure the Manufacturer’s Warranty starts on the date that you receive your car, and not any time before
  • Be suspicious and explore with your gut instinct (and for me, that was can I trust my own kind of people?)
  • Don’t sign anything until you receive your vehicle – if you do, you still have the right to cancel the contract if you do not have the vehicle yet
  • Do not get any accessories or rust-proofing/undercoating packages at the dealership as after-market places can do it for much cheaper (and better)
  • Try to get a deadline set so that if they cannot find a car for you by a certain date, the deal is no longer valid
  • Who says car shopping or buying a new car is fun/exciting?!

randominspirations and I went to my co-CEO’s home because we had some meeting appointment with him. It was evening, and we saw a SUV in his driveway. I also knew that he had a black one. We knocked on the glass pane that was next to their door. His wife came and opened the door for us and let us in. We sat down to enjoy a dinner meal together while chatting away. Their children were sleeping and their dog was highly affectionate. At one point, his wife left the table to greet some relatives (or friends) and were chatting away really loudly in Chinese, as many Chinese do. He made some sort of remark about his “tai tai – wife” and her Chinese friends/relatives. We also learned that his brother owned a car dealership which is why had so many SUVs along with other cars. Before we left, one of their children came down and started talking to us. I was impressed as she was only 1 year old (14 months?). — Later, I ended up walking to some sort of gymnasium and it somehow automatically transported people in it to a building that belonged to UT. I saw a familiar face, which I didn’t know at first but I didn’t want to let on. So I asked, Are you fundaisies, happydaises, meaomeao, etc.  to see if she would identify with her cyber identity. She was puzzled and asked me about 3D. That was when I knew it was Vicky, the girl who moved to England to take dance school and came back a few years later to Vancouver. But, why was she in Toronto?
