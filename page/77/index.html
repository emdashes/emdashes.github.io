i was in a plane, watching what was going on, and realised that the people in front of me were preparing to crash land in the ocean. everyone was so scared that no one was going to make it. turns out, that the plane touched land onto the waters very smoothly. momentarily, we relaxed. however, to our dismay, the plane quickly took on water and before some people had the chance to unbuckle their seatbelts, water leaked in and the pressure pushed the windows in and some of us were able to get out of the plane before it went under. and yet, somehow, not all of the plane went under or we found a piece where people could sit in and wait. luckily, a plane was near by, landed and started loading people on the plane. i was near that back of the plane so we had to wait a long time, until the plane started to sag from the water and then they knew that they had to hurry. so, they loaded people from the back and front at the same time.

by the end of the ordeal, we were fast friends with those around us. and by the time we arrived at the airport, i was really glad to have gotten off the plane. i was scared for my friend jen tho. after exchanging contact info (in which i wrote my address to be 280 phillip street), she still had to fly from another plane to another destination, so we saw her off from the viewer’s gallery. what’s really weird about that gallery is that you could technically get onto the plane before it took off when the security guard isn’t looking b/c of the gap between the plane’s doors and the gallery.

fantasy-world

impressions on msn != reflections of the real you
or me.
leaving the cyberworld and connecting through many wires
two friends exclaim
that there is a trace of a chinese accent in my spoken english
the punchline? i’m a “jook-sing”
born and raised in canada
ironic –
how there are those who criticize my chinese noting that my english-speaking accent
is littered throughout
i can’t exist to please both worlds;
how oil and vinegar will emulsify

perks of living in cyberspace
being part of a surreal fantasy
and living a fairy-tale life
and they lived happily ever after.
the end.

baad msn + wild imagination = trouble
msn now tries to deceive me
by playing mindgames with me
thus blurring the lines of reality
and fantasy.

perks to add to my life collection of stories.
i love my life.

Mr. Poppyman

News update: For those who live in a hole (ahem, that would be you ppl living in “Onterrible” as my collegue says), today is Remembrance Day; therefore, it’s a public holiday in BC. In the past 3 months, we’ve also had major natural disasters including forest fires (much like California did a few weeks ago), water shortages, floods (yes, people were stranded, some died, and houses were soaked), and wind storms (80 km/hr). Yes, leaves do change colours on our trees as well (though admittedly, not as beautiful as Gatineau).

My apologies for the lack of updates in the last while. Aside from contract/work deadlines, the past month has been filled with non-stop excitement. With acquaintances from Ontario, who have since become fast friends, I have finally done my first all-you-can eat sushi since coming back, ate at Cactus Club, explored and gushed over a game-development campus that offered free arcades amongst other appeals (wow, beat a guy at Marvel vs. Capcom just by button-mashing =p), drove like never before, saw all of Vancouver in one-day, and enjoyed each others’ company with food and laughter.

The past weekend, namely Saturday, was jammed-packed! On Friday night, Ben arrived from Seattle and so, the first stop, was real bubbletea. Unwound with a downloaded copy of Serendipity at home (no, I’ve never seen it before til that day; it’s a weird film) and some lime/lemon flavoured popcorn from Mexico (smells like fruitloops, but it’s actually quite good =p).

Halo9 seems to have described Saturday succintly. Highlights? – along w Abbas, Chan, Chung, and Lee, we drove through/past Chinatown, E.Hastings (think: 1 corner, 5 police cars), glanced at Gastown, walked around Canada Place, Waterfront, gaped at the base of the Top of Vancouver Revolving Restaurant (mental note, lunch starts at $22), witnessed the “voluntary mugging” of a friend by the Poppyman ($10 for “2” poppies for “children” – both which broke w/in 2 hrs), rode the seabus to Lonsdale Quay, lunch @ McD’s, walked around the market (where a big fish grossed someone out), detoured onto the scenic drive through Stanley Park, cruised through the Lion’s Gate Bridge, hiked over Lynn Valley Canyon via the free suspension bridge and across the Twin Falls trail, visited Cleveland’s Dam – where an episode of Dark Angel was filmed, drove to the base of Grouse Mountain (@ $22.95 per person, it was not worth it), forgot to visit the Salmon Hatchery on the way to Granville Island, redeemed my Kid’s Market midway tickets from eons back (15 = minipopper), enjoyed a scrumptious wine and seafood dinner at the Sandbar, walked off the alcohol and food, critiqued students’ work at the Emily Carr Institute of Art + Design, filled ourselves with Mario’s Gelato (mental note: this one doesn’t allow for 1/2 scoops), and met a car on autopilot at the red light which almost killed chung. Thankfully, everyone got home safely and we all had a good rest after a long day.

Thanks to the people at church who made Ben feel welcome. He really felt that because he actually remembered Daniel and Clarence’s names and found them welcoming. Thanks guys, for making a positive impression of BAC :).

Parents brought car for check-up today and reports: muffler has hole (ha! explains the noise and the difficulty we had w the car on Saturday!).

O, M had a farm!

If I told you that one of my distant neighbours used to own a goat for a pet, and that in my backyard, we have 1 cherry tree, 2 plum trees, 1 nectarine tree, 2 pear trees, 2 apple trees, and 1 bamboo tree, you’d likely think that we lived in the rural areas of the city. Nevertheless, you’re welcome to visit to pick your own fruits…including wildberries.

Since I’ve been poetizing in the last l’il while, I may as well continue the trend.

Psalm 8 (New Living Translation):

O LORD, don’t rebuke me in your anger!
Don’t discipline me in your rage!

Your arrows have struck deep,
and your blows are crushing me.

Because of your anger, my whole body is sick;
my health is broken because of my sins.

My guilt overwhelms me–
it is a burden too heavy to bear.

My wounds fester and stink
because of my foolish sins.

I am bent over and racked with pain.
My days are filled with grief.

A raging fever burns within me,
and my health is broken.

I am exhausted and completely crushed.
My groans come from an anguished heart.

You know what I long for, Lord;
you hear my every sigh.

My heart beats wildly, my strength fails,
and I am going blind.

My loved ones and friends stay away, fearing my disease.
Even my own family stands at a distance.

Meanwhile, my enemies lay traps for me;
they make plans to ruin me.
They think up treacherous deeds all day long.

But I am deaf to all their threats.
I am silent before them as one who cannot speak.

I choose to hear nothing,
and I make no reply.

For I am waiting for you, O LORD.
You must answer for me, O Lord my God.

I prayed, “Don’t let my enemies gloat over me
or rejoice at my downfall.”

I am on the verge of collapse,
facing constant pain.

But I confess my sins;
I am deeply sorry for what I have done.

 
 

My enemies are many;
they hate me though I have done nothing against them.

They repay me evil for good
and oppose me because I stand for the right.

Do not abandon me, LORD.
Do not stand at a distance, my God.

Come quickly to help me, O Lord my savior.

Shakespeare

Do you remember studying Hamlet in high school or university? I just found another piece I wrote in Grade 9 (on Hamlet). I didn’t realise how well Shakespeare wrote – the symbolism and hidden meanings, with the “intricacies of the english language”, as j.w would say. Anyhow, if you remember who Ophelia and what happens in Hamlet, you’ll likely understand this better (if not, just go to sparknotes.com for a brief summary):

OPHELIA’S FLOWERS

“There’s rosemary, that’s for remembrance,”
Ophelia whispered.
Given to her lover as a token of remembrance.
Did she not know that her brother, Laertes, was not Hamlet?
Her dear brother was to be remembered in his death.

“And there is pansies, that’s for thoughts.”
The pansies given along with rosemary
With blossoms of rich colour
Of velvet texture,
As sentiments of her love.

“There’s fennel for you, and columbines.”
Symbols of flattery and unfaithfulness
Flowers to the Queen
Ophelia freely bestowed.

“There’s rue for you.”
A meaningful flower presented to the King.
Herb of grace the flower is also known
WIth a name preaching of repentance and regret.

“There’s a daisy”
With a saffron centre
Surrounded with petals of pale white.
The memories of forsaken love
Overwhelmed Ophelia.

“I would give you some violets, but they withered all when my father died.”
She pointed out with a sigh.
Her violets wilted with the loss of her father and lover.
Violets of faithfulness and love.

Willows, trees of sadness.
Nettles, weeds that conjure pain, poison, and betrayal.
Nettles pierced her heart.
Crow-flowers, flowers of dejection.
Long-purples, and of daisies
Intertwined into the garland of blosssoms.
The willows wept
For Ophelia.

 

Incidentally, did you know that the name Ophelia means “immortal one”?

On my knees

Sometimes, I wonder how I can possibly do it. And, then I realise that I can’t, at least not on my own. When I hear their stories, even when I may not know them too well, divine compassion and love overwhelms me – two emotions that bring me to intercession. I often hold back (self or geographic restraints) from the urges to embrace them with a hug, wishing that perhaps it would ease the pain and hurt. In times like these, I get a tiny glimpse of what Jesus must have felt during his ministry on earth. He knew what they were going through, but He couldn’t do anything for them because they chose not to ask or receive His help. And ytI feel the frustration, the helplessness, but all I can do is pray, wait, and hope for the best; that God will change their minds, that God would change His mind.

—

Questions about the UBC campus:

  • Is there a UBC entrance sign where tourists and/or students can take pictures?
  • Why are there so many sk8rs @ UBC? At UW, it’s bladers, here skateboarding is the preferred means of transportation.

—

While waiting for the bus, someone approaches me and asks, “Do you speak Chinese?” Upon discovering that I spoke Cantonese, he excitedly engaged me into conversation, which consisted primarily of his misgivings of UBC/Vancouver like, “why isn’t there a single sign on campus that ubc has for ppl to take pictures with? look at the University of Beijing! people line up so that they can have a picture with the sign.” And, “what’s so good about UBC? it’s so famous in hk! there’s nothing on this campus…and the food at the SUB is so not good..is there any place else on campus that’s good?” (i was thinking about telling him that perhaps it’s the reputation and not the facilities). I tried to explain to him that at night, UBC isn’t really much and during the day, the ocean-view from campus is really nice. But he wasn’t too keen on scenery. In fact, when I suggested that he should go to Granville Island, Stanley Park and explained to him that they were good tourist places especially for scenery, it didn’t appeal to him at all. He wanted places that resembled Causeway Bay, HK – places bustling with people, places to shop and to play arcades! At that point, I wanted to tell him that people visit Vancouver often because of its scenic appeal; if he wanted to shop and play arcades, stay in HK, as he would save a lot more money that way. But, since he was already here and since my bus arrived,I just sent him to Metrotown/Metropolis, the largest mall in this area – bustling with people and with a place to play arcades at: Playdium. *shakes head*: what a waste.
