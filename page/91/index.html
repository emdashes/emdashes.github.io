I give thanks for Thanksgiving…

Grace? God’s Riches At Christ’s Expense.

During a dinner that a seeking friend (same one as mentioned elsewhere in cyberspace) and I had this week (“out” as I didn’t feel like cooking for the both of us after a long tiring day), we were discussing religion and I asked him what he thought Christianity was all about. To my “unsurprise”, the first thing that popped into his mind were “the 10 commandments”. He saw a new spin to Christianity as I tried to relate last week’s CCF Bible Study to this conversation. Jesus said, “if you love me, you’ll obey my commands” (paraphrased –see the book of John). The whole point of Christianity, which differs from many other religions is that we aren’t saved through deeds (1 bad action + 2 good actions=0), but rather, through grace. An emphasis that Catholic churches sometimes miss out (hence, purguratory or penance — to “purge” one of Wrongdoings prior to approaching the God). I learned a lot about other religions that night through my friend, and i thoroughly enjoyed our conversation.

I leave you with Paul’s words (Romans 3:20):
“For no one can ever be made right in God’s sight by doing what his law commands. For the more we know God’s law, the clearer it becomes that we aren’t obeying it.”

1 Corinthians 13

The New International Version says this about love beginning in verse 8 (to verse 13): “Love never fails. But where there are prophecies, they will cease; where there are tongues, they will be stilled; where there is knowledge, it will pass away. For we know in part and we prophesy in part, but when perfection comes, the imperfect disappears. When I was a child, I talked like a child, I thought like a child, I reasoned like a child. When I became a man, I put childish ways behind me. Now we see but a poor reflection as in a mirror; then we shall see face to face. Now I know in part; then I shall know fully, even as I am fully known. And now these three remain: faith, hope and love. But the greatest of these is love.”

A riddle? Not really. One interpretation applies the techniques of logos (appeal of reasoning) to Paul’s (author) words. Paul suggests 4 things that may have been seen as important to the church of Corinth. Through the process of elimination, the conclusion is that love is the only thing that remains. Paul implies that prophecies and tongues (languages) are only temporal; thus, only faith, hope, and love remain. Hebrews 11:1 defines faith as “being sure of what we hope for and certain of what we do not see.” Hope, is then a desire of something that’s to come. Now because a Christian will not need to have faith in God once s/he sees Him b/c then He will be seen face to face and all will know that He is real. Secondly, believers, both in Paul’s and present day, hope for Christ’s return; therefore, hope will be fulfilled in His Second Coming. Thus, only love remains, which is then why Paul labels love as being the greatest.

I’m not sure if I’ve eloquently put what I realised into understandable words, but if that made sense to you at all, read 1 Corinthians 13:4-13 again, but this time in the New Living Translation. This version just throws the whole interpretation out the window as verse thirteen says, “There are three things that will endure–faith, hope, and love–and the greatest of these is love.” Mind you though, NLT is a more paraphrased (translated) version than NIV (which is an “in-btwn” of literal meaning (KJV) and paraphrase (NLT, CEV, TLB)). So, now, I don’t know what to think….

Mid-Autumn Fest weekend

did end up in Mississauga and Toronto (to actually GO sightseeing! woo! :} )! On Friday afternoon, DD kindly drove me out to ‘Sauga so I could meet up w Mel, my hs friend, who’s on a co-op double term there. I feel so sorry for her; ‘Sauga is so uneventful–no wonder the three weekends that she’s been there, she’s been going out to TO…I think nobody can really live in ‘sauga w/o access to a car for the bus/transit system is sooo ineffecient! Can you believe that the last bus on a evening during the weekend is 20h30?! I know for a fact that in Ottawa and Vancouver, transit systems are generally running until at least midnight, if not later!

On Friday and Sunday, we just sat around a chilled: Vegged on the couch w Fear Factor, caught up on each other’s lives and planned for Saturday. Ah yehs, I forgot one crucial afterthought: I ended up finished half (yes, you read me correctly, that’s 1/2 of a whole) a Dominion’s chicken, some potato salad, rice, and a bowl of Cream of Mushroom soup that night; boy, was I stuffed! We left the house by 9h00 on Saturday morning to catch the GO train to Union Station ($8.60 round trip). That was my first time on the GO train; apparently, it’s almost exactly like the West Coast Express in ‘couver b/c on the way there, we actually passed a GO train that had old(?) WCexpress cars attached to them. The thing I didn’t notice on the GO train was the workstations, and plugs for computers, but I guess it’s also that the fare is slighty cheaper for the distance travelled.

Thankfully, I wore really comfie shoes to Mel’s this weekend, else my foot would’ve been quite sore. We first tried to find our way to the ROM from Union w this really un-detailed map, but heheh, she ended up looking at the Art Museum of Ontario instead, so we arrived there. Funny though, because we ended up walking in a circle. Ah wellz :). At least we “found” the MuchMusic building that we couldn’t locate on the Net (it’s on John and near McCaul). Thus, we ended up walking up Queen’s Park, past the UT St. George Campus (woohoo, now I know where that is too!), to um…I can’t remember the name! Dundas? and Bloor St. W to the ROM. Boo, I just missed the Images of Salvation display at the ROM. The ROM is semi-interesting; not interesting enough for me to pay them $18. Did you know that it’s a pay-as-you-can sorta thing? Apparently, on Saturday, it was free! :). ‘cept there really was no signs or anything advertising it, but Melody found it online so YAAY! for her (I can’t seem to find that anywhere) :P. In actuality, I enjoyed the Ontario Science Centre a lot more as it was more interactive and more appliable “oh-i-c” exhibits w less “artifacts” (which I went in 1999 for free due to my membership to all science centres in the world at that time).

So, after a big lunch at McD’s, we walked onto Bloor and headed towards Spadina and Dupont…to Casa Loma. ‘Twas a nice walk (tho long!) — Mel complained that I walk too fast hahah, well, all I wanted to do was get there….we didn’t imagine that we had to climb up a hill and some odd steps before reaching this “castle”. Thankfully, we had 2 dollars off, so the admission came to $8. However, the self-audio guide included w the admission is very lengthy and the quality unsurpasses the one at Alcatraz island. No wonder “the Rock”‘s is an award-winning one!

We took many pix, but until I get permission to post them publicly, if you wish to see them, contact me. My mom commented that if I told her that we were in Europe, she’d believe me :). The castle is nice, I admit it has the typical associations with a “castle”: secret tunnels and passages, turrets, nice gardens, etc. etc. However, I find that the Boldt’s Castle is prettier (more romantic?:P) :). By the time we finished our pictures and tour, the castle was closing, so we walked back to Union and took the GO train home. During the bus ride back, we last-minutedly decided to risk the bus schedule and eat at the AMC plaza in Oakville. BUT East Side Mario’s (which M has never been to) had a 1/2 hr line up and w the wait, we’d never make the last bus home. Thus, we settled for BP (boston-pizza, not me!), which turned out well b/c Mel never been there before, even tho it’s across the street from her at home! :).

Stayed up talking and did the same the next morning/afternoon til DD came to “retrieve” me :P. Hopefully, M and S will visit ME next :). That night, I had an interesting dream, in which someone revealed to me what I already “knew” in my mind. In reality, as i was revealing my dream the following day to another source, I found out that it was true! Scary…

PS. Praise God! He answered my prayers 🙂 The professor extended my deadline for my assignments due to textbook problems til next week!
