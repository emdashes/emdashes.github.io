Freedom…and lessons in ginger rinse

Chinese confinement, which means a day out with Little Bean (who was on her best behaviour) 🙂 The midwife weighed her at 8lbs 4oz, a great sign that she’s gaining instead of losing (from 6lb 7oz to 5lb something, then after some correction and supplement, back up to gaining weight and surpassing her birth weight as of two weeks, which means, we can start using the next size up in diapers! Over the past week, We have noticed that she’s grown in length too, as her legs now stick out in her newborn outfit, when it was still hiding last week! She’s also given us a number of poo-free nights, laughed in her sleep, started following objects (or at least looking as if she’s interested in them), and we’ve noticed her started to blow milk bubbles/drool more, as of today. I guess the bibs will be coming in handy soon. Over the past month I’ve taken a handful or two of showers with ginger rinse. It’s kind of funny that I’ve finally figured out what works best, when it’s the end of the month now:

  • the best rinse that you can feel the effects is from ginger itself, not from boiling ginger peel
  • ginger rinse is best when it’s hot, and fresh (or hot from frozen). After a day out or so, it starts to smell off…
  • be sure not to get any in your eyes or open wounds, as it tends to sting, a lot

Baby predictions

Today marks the end of my 2.5 days of vacation before maternity leave officially starts! I had plans to sleep in, but sadly, every morning so far, I’ve woken up around 9 AM to extreme heat, pelvic pain, and an extreme urge to pee. I also make sure j.w gets up too, since he has to get to work. Actually, embarrassingly enough, when I have to go these days, I’ve learned that I shouldn’t delay or ignore the urge, so I jolt and bolt to the washroom (otherwise, I risk wetting my pants :|). This, paired with menstrual-like cramps on Monday, and what I suspect might be the “bloody show” in the last two days (though really it’s not really a one-time thing if that’s it), is perhaps a sign that D-day is coming sooner rather than later (D being delivery or doomsday, or both). I think most people had a good time at the baby shower, and it seemed that despite two different groups of people (Toronto and KW) with some age differences (from masters students to older married couples with and without a toddler in tow), people seemed to find conversations that they could engage in; I felt especially blessed to have that many friends and family who would come to pray for and with us. As I mentioned to another friend, everyone’s excitement is encouraging, and will likely help get me through labour (and the days where joy might not be as abundant)! Coincidentally, most of us ended up wearing blue or neutral / grey tops – perhaps a sign of the gender of the baby to come? And, maybe it is a boy. Afterall, we found out yesterday that for some reason,  bébé stops moving whenever Clare wants to touch it, even though it was moving throughout the evening! Actually, despite that D-Day seems to be looming in the near distance, this baby is still very active and doesn’t seem to be lacking room at all (contrary to literature), but the body parts that stick out seem a lot more prominent now; I’ve noticed this especially after eating watermelon.     Full-term

    This week marks another milestone: 37 weeks of pregnancy, which means I’m full-term; that is if I deliver the baby now, it’s no longer a pre-mature baby even if it hasn’t reached week 40 yet. I’ve been feeling very blessed, with unexpected gifts from various people and even more help around the house by Daddy-to-be! I even see God’s provision through my coworkers’ generous gift basket, which contained items that’s been on my outstanding checklist like receiving blankets and a baby manicure set (that they didn’t even know about!). As I tie up loose ends at work and at home, I still don’t feel adequately prepared, but a Bible verse has popped up in my readings, which seems very suitable for the upcoming days – I’ve been trying to commit the bolded text to memory, so that it will get me through labour!:

    But [the Lord] said to me ,”My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore I will boast all the more gladly of my weaknesses, so that the power of Christ may rest upon me. – 2 Corinthians 12:9-10 (ESV)

    Just over a month to go…

    If I ever get pregnant, I’d want it to be as painless as possible ; in fact, maybe we’ll just adopt!”  (I have a low pain threshold) Yet, somehow, over the course of pregnancy, my thinking has evolved from wanting a scheduled/elected C-section to an epidural at a minimum to: “I’m hoping for a natural birth if at all possible….”  while keeping an open mind for pain relief choices/options. And with just over a month to go (less to reach full-term), here’s what I’ve continued to discover:

    • My wardrobe has shrunk down to 1/3 of its size, giving us a lot more closest space; maternity clothes from aunts’ generation still seem many sizes too big/wide (and I don’t think it ever will work at this point), but low-rise skinny jeans/pants still fit, if left partially buttoned with the hair-tie technique or with a belly band
    • Pelvic bone pain/pressure increase is normal (though at times, walking hurts as a result)
    • Baby still has room to hiccup and move around, to the amusement of j.w, and Grace and Vivian today
    • Dad baby books are much more light-hearted and easier to read than mommy books (but with similar content). Why are ours so much more serious?
    • Belly sometimes gets in the way of putting on socks, pants, and tying my shoe, and especially when going to public washrooms where small stall doors open inwards
    Outstanding checklist items:
    • Start packing a hospital bag
    • Complete laundry for baby items
    • Paint baby room, put up Blik decal
    • Finish basement
    • Tidy up and get the house ready for baby and guests
    • Then, there is the task or obligations of traditional customs like making vinegar ginger soup Long-boiled soup isn’t a forté of mine to begin with and I dread having to reboil a concoction daily
    —- Side note: I spent a day in Toronto with Grace this past weekend to see Kim’s Convenience at the Distillery District, a 90-minute, 5-cast (7? character) play that peeks into the life of a Korean family, touching upon cultural and generational stereotypes and ideas between the first-generation parents and their second-generation children, often with moments that I could identify with a comedic approach and some tear-jerking scenes. It was quite appropriate for Father’s Day weekend, as it was primarily around the dynamics between the father and his daughter (played by Grace’s friend, also named Grace) and  the son, including how they express love to each other, reminding me of the story of the prodigal son and how our Father’s love is unconditional. The performance was completely worth the $50, which is more than what I would normally spend on the theatre, but my excuse was that it would probably be one of the last pre-parent outings for awhile…Plus, the performers (Ins Choi – also screenwright, Jean Yoon – who was stopped by an older couple as she cycled by) seemed approachable and pleasant to have a casual chat with. If it stops in Vancouver next May, I’m going to recommend it to my Vancouver friends, especially the Tungs!]]>
