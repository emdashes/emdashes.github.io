    Troubled Spirit

    Thus, I blog; I share encouraging answered prayers, of the short prayer I offered up towards the heavens in the afternoon of the Columbia incident. No, I did not follow the lead of the American leaders, praying for the already lost, but rather for the families, and for hope to those affected. Yet, my spirit only paused a short moment to wonder the fate of the perished souls, grieving for the lives lost for eternity. Romans 1:28, 31 correctly emphasizes the plight of man, “Furthermore, since they did not think it worthwhile to retain the knowledge of God, he gave them over to a depraved mind, to do what ought not to be done…they are senseless, faithless, heartless, ruthless.” Senseless: “mo zhi” (as translated in Cantonese). Day in, day out, media shows clips of violence, sex, hatred, bitterness and war against fellow man, and the hearts of the viewers are hardened, detached, distant, and desensitized to the graphical images and the implications that follow them. And, although the tragedy occupied over 10 hours of television airtime on virtually every channel, just two days later, the war on Iraq was once again the top headlines on CNN. In the brief minute where my spirit paused to ponder, incomphrensibly, I grieved for these 7 people even more so than over my grandfather’s death, for I knew his eternity was secured in Christ Jesus.

    Some days passed without further thought or incident, but on a particular day, as I was routinely cleaning out my inbox, an e-mail forward captivated my attention. Steve Green, a Christian artist shared about his friendship with Rick Husband, the captain of the mission and crew on board the Columbia. As a Christian, Rick prayed for the crew before departure, and actively witnessed to those working around him. As I read those words, a familiar sense of doubt crept into my mind, for afterall, aren’t e-mail forwards 99% of the time spam, junk, or urban legends? On the verge of dismissing another rumour, I quickly typed in “Rick Husband Steve Green” on Google, the ever-reliable search engine. After 5 minutes of searching for valid links, I ended up back at CNN. This time, I read the transcript of an interview with Connie Chung on her Tonight show. Steve did not only receive an e-mail from outer space from Rick, but as a consequence of what happened, he also openly got a chance to proclaim Rick’s faith in God, and the audience had the opportunity to hear a clip of God of Wonders, a song that Rick’s wife chose to play to the astronauts to wake them up in the morning, a song with such suiting lyrics (I do imagine that I’ll sing this song with a different perspective the next time its played at worship)! His children will likely cherish the last videos, prepared by their father so they could “do” devotions with them each day that he was gone.

    Thus, I am certain that lives are changed and touched by Rick Husband, Mike Anderson, and another astronaut’s living testimonies even though they are no longer on earth. And by that, all men will know that they are His disciples. And, although I had no intentions of dedicating an entire journal entry to this incident, as I would be following the footsteps of those before me, I end with no regrets.

    Happy (Chinese) New Year!

    Last night, CCF had a bible study. What struck me most, was that I was in no hurry for it to finish this time–even though we went 1/2 hour overtime. I overheard of our group members noting that as the bible study came to a close, and I thought to myself, “Wow, we’re in such a rush to get through the material these days, even if there is “substance” to them. In Jesus’ time and in certain countries of the world, people crave to hear God’s word, even if it means sitting through lunch or dinner.” I was encouraged tho, because as I stayed behind to fellowship with the people (note that next to everyone went home for Chinese New Year, so the group size was VERY small–60 or less? perfect size: reminds me of the past years. I actually stayed til the end until everyone was cleaning up–haven’t done that for a LONG time now), one brother noted that he actually learnt something in bible study this time. Then, he started cracking up because what he said sounded bad, but I was encouraged nevertheless, as he even admitted that most bible studies bore him to death. When I asked him what he learnt, I knew that he really thought about what was “passed” out as “food for thought” in his group. As for myself, I took a new perspective in looking at the structure of Ephesians 4. Because I normally read Ephesians 4 either on my Palm or in the Bible, I never noticed that the entire chapter echoes Colossians 3:12-14 where it says, “Therefore, as God’s chosen people, holy and dearly loved, clothe yourselves with compassion, kindness, humility, gentleness and patience. Bear with each other and forgive whatever grievances you may have against one another. Forgive as the Lord forgave you. And over all these virtues put on love, which binds them all together in perfect unity.” Like two kaiser buns holding a sandwich together, Ephesians 4 begins with, “Be completely humble and gentle; be patient, bearing with one another in love.” (v2) and ends with ““Be kind and compassionate to one another, forgiving each other, just as in Christ God forgave you.” (v32). I thought that was kinda neat. And, to those that were in my bible study group (Deb, if you’re reading this, that means you), my apologies: we didn’t study Eph 4 in the bible study at the retreat in 2001, but rather Colossians 3:12-17. I realised my mistake shortly after I found related notes on my Palm regarding Christian community. Oops. Actually, I didn’t realise that there were actually two different passages on “bearing” with one another–I remember that in 2001, I recalled those words sticking out from the page, “Bear with each other”…and thinking, “in trying to love someone, the first step is to “bear” with them…”

    Anyhow, I’m likely making no sense to you, as the reader, but that’s okay, because I was just trying to articulate to/remind myself what I learnt on Friday. Ah yes, fellowshipping w Pearlo, DanTe, and Ricky afterwards was a new and different, but nevertheless positive experience. Ahh, the joys of having “intimate” conversations with smaller groups. Yep =)–so who won? Lakers?! Hahaha.

    Oh, 23 minutes over bedtime. And I gotta finish my readings too…heheh.
