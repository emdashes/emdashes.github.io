A Twist in the Story

And the plot thickens. Waking up daily to get to work by 8:30-9:00 means that I need to be out of bed by 6:15-6:45 (and if I’m VERY lucky, 7:15). Argh. I’ve never needed to take 1.5 hrs to commute to anywhere before on a regular basis — be it school or work (and I thought living in Orléans and working in Ottawa West was bad enough). Ah wells, I’m really blessed for having to have found a job within 2 weeks of being unemployed :).

Good news, I’m back in ON for one week. Shame that Marcy hasta work and can’t come down to see me before i leave again O_o. On the other hand, my first and only interview thus far is coming up in less than 14 hours. I don’t even know how I got it to begin with since I do not match the qualifications of the job description much at all. God’s sense of humour? Nevertheless, I thank God and the experience of being interviewed would be a good thing, especially since I’ve wanted this job at this company ever since I was in RPW. Well, if I do my part in preparing, and if this position is for me, God will see it through. And, of course, working in Waterloo won’t be so bad afterall 🙂 (even though it means that I’ll be moving everything back again if I do end up there).

it's a small, small world

…Though the mountains divide
And the oceans are wide
It’s a small world afterall…

It never ceases to amaze me the number of people I get to meet and end up knowing in my short lifespan here on earth. Life is sure full of surprises! I remember her–tanned and yet, swollen lip from taking a hit from a softball–sitting down and joining us for lunch but her name escaped me. I wasn’t sure her name; I had to ask my lunch buddy afterwards. Yet, who would’ve imagined that within a month, my family would receive a copy of the family tree in the mail. And, I, being one who enjoys reading up on names and such, scanned the booklet for a familiar name beyond my cousins and uncles/aunts–without much expectations. My eyes stopped when I recognised a first name. Could it? Would it be her? A quick calculation of her birthyear seemed plausible. How many Chinese girls had the name of Phoebe? Not very many. Thus, taking a risk, I e-mail her. She affirms her relationship with her parents and questions who I was, and thus I explain to her my story. We share the same great-grandparents! Isn’t that something? I’ve met her, talked with her, dined with her, and never truly realised that our acquaintance-ship extends to much more 🙂

Everyday, I browse through people’s blogs as if browsing through a newspaper. Something catches my attention every so often, and today it was how one friend’s (Boy) parents knows my other friend’s (Joshie) parents from almost 3 decades ago! Boy’s parents knew them in time for their wedding–before l’il Joshie was born! Who would’ve imagined?

True Freedom

Well, the wedding was quite…different, but fun nevertheless. I guess having a Western style wedding is quite nice especially in a casual setting and especially if one can escape the fun and games of traumatic Chinese-style weddings. The bouqet and the garter were not even tossed/thrown! The wedding ceremony for about 150 witnesses was outdoors, set outside of St. George’s Junior School (some posh private school) — thankfully, the weather co-operated! It was a beautiful day to be having a wedding. What struck me as being odd was that the officiator said a prayer of blessing for them and the emcee said grace before the meal…which really was a sad reminder for me of what they once held dear. I saw some relatives that came for the wedding, and saw the faces of those who were once my Chinese School classmates. The ceremony was followed with some hors d’ouevres and the dinner reception (along w dancing) at St. George’s Senior School. The head table seating arrangement at the front of the room totally reminded me of Harry Potter :p Wow, to grow up w my cos from when we were in primary school to til now, when she’s at the age of marriage! Time sure flies…

Today in Sunday School (where only Becky and 4 other adults attended), Ivan really was the engaging sharer in our class. I don’t know who he is, but his comments are highly thought-provoking. The topic of suffering often lends itself to the freedom of choice. Many people argue that if God is the all-knowing Creator, it also means that we, as people, don’t have choice. Often, I used the traffic light analogy. Say that I was in a building overseeing traffic and could control traffic (and thus, people’s responses) by switching the lights. If I saw a speeding car coming towards the intersection, I could make sure that opposing traffic be out of harm’s way by making the traffic light red. However, it’s up to the driver of the car to stop at the red light to avoid hitting the oncoming car.

Ivan noted that our perception of choice is marred by the fall of man, and therefore we don’t have true freedom of choice — that only Adam and Eve truly had to choose between good and evil, because their lives were not tainted by the presence of evil. He shared with us the analogy that made more sense and in turn, made me think of the Matrix analogy. However, just like an alcoholic, one has the choice to drink – be it wine or beer or cocktails – he has that choice but he only has limited freedom to choose. As one addicted to alcohol, he cannot choose to drink or not to drink. Similarly, in the Matrix, Neo only had 2 choices in 2 situations: to take the red or blue pill and to take the left or right door (to save Trinity or to save Zion). He had perfect freedom to choose but his options were limited. Agh, don’t know where I’m going with this, but anyhow, yeah :).

Belonging

Well, it’s settled. My sister has lent me three of her outfits to choose from–I’ll likely end up picking one that fits me lengthwise the most {i lied, i wore one of mine}. The youth (and young adults) had a praise and worship session tonight…which I definitely needed getting useta. I forgot how LOUD music is at BAC, to the pt where I couldn’t really hear whether I was singing or not :|, but overall, it was quite good. I enjoyed the harmonizing of the vocals up front…and James was quite an engaging speaker (minus the really long prayer/quiet time which I almost–but didn’t–dozed off, but he picked that up too in the audience so he concluded w some hip n’ happenin’ songs!).

I’m really glad that my friend Mel came out w me tonight though because it’s weird to be a silent observer…a distant outsider. I don’t belong. We/I just kinda lurked back in the corner observing everyone talk in their cliques and just watching the dynamics of the room. Hmm. I guess being away for so long has that effect on you…I don’t know most of the people there, and the people that I did know, I don’t anymore…kinda sad really. I know why I prefer small churches now…to this very day, HCCC and OCAC has been the best at making ppl feel not only welcome, but right at home. I see a fine line between the two and it makes all the difference in the world. I know I’m guilty of doing that: I may welcome ppl when they’re new, but often, I don’t do a good job at making them feel a part of the community/circle. Well, now that I know, that’s a mental note for myself to work on…hopefully, for the better!

Time flies when you're having fun

See_Lai Alert: For those 3-2-1 Penguin fans out there, Wal-Mart has the latest two DVDS packaged in one price — $17.88 + Tax (regular retail price) … works out to be about $9 per DVD (as opposed to the regular prices of $20+ sold at Christian bookstores).

The program for CCF this past week was labelled “Worship Night”. I thought the title was slightly misleading as it didn’t really have much to do with personal worship, but moreso a testimonial sharing night on the “storms” in the lives of those who shared, based on the passage of Peter and Jesus walking on water (Matthew 14:22-24). Some people presented commentaries on their interpretation of that passage, whereas others looked at the story with a fresh perspective, at a completely different angle. The passage itself was quite suiting as the weather on that particular day was quite stormy in itself. Yet, lurking beyond the storm clouds, one could see crystal clear blue sky. And, that’s how life’s storms are–once they blow over, you’re back onto solid ground. I think Aaron Jeffrey words it best in their song, “After the Rain”. The chorus goes like this: After the rain
You can look to the sky again
The clouds will give way
To the light of the sun
After the rain
You know that you’ve made it through
And you’ll finally see the joy from the pain
After the rain From the sharing, I realised that I was not alone in my struggles with CS. Everything that Ainat shared about, I totally connected…’cept she’s one step above me, she’s actually a CS major (props to her!). Like her, I chose to study some CS courses partially because of parental pressue, and partly out of pure interest. Almost immediately after graduation though, I seemed to have struggled with pride creeping up in me every so often, just because I made it through with more than “just” and Arts degree (yes, I think the Asian ideology brainwashed me). And yet, I know for a certain fact that without God’s grace in providing me so many dedicated people to help me build a solid foundation in the basics and an understanding in the complex, and His mercy when it came to writing exams, I would’ve never ever made it at all. I can’t even count how many times I’ve talked about quitting (to others), prayed about quitting (obviously it wasn’t in His will now that I see in hindsight), felt that I would be forced to quit, and finally just plain wanted to quit. Hmm, wait, that sounds familiar…sounds like my struggle in pre-health/opt, cept I actually did go through with “quitting” (which I don’t regret for a second actually).

Wow, there’s exactly 2 weeks before I leave this place…possibly forever long-term. Woah, I just realised that I almost lived in Waterloo for 1 year. That’s scary. But, I’ve never realised how much fun one can have at school when one’s not studying! In about a week’s time, there’s the church picnic to look forward to–if I can find people to go with me…and well, it sounds “oh-so-appealing”: sandy beach, water, trails, grass, shelter, park admission fee…hmm, why does that sound SOOOO familiar?! I sure hope it doesn’t have mosquitoes :|!
