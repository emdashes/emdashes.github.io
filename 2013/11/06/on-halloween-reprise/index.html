On Halloween (Reprise)

Posted byEmily November 6, 2013
3 Comments on On Halloween (Reprise)

A local business’ Halloween model, as posted on their BBM Channel[/caption] While our babe is still young and doesn’t understand the implications of Halloween, a couple of parents have brought up the topic of Halloween and its influence on young children. Most of our culture today see Halloween as a fun day (much like Zombie walks on other days of the year), not a day to celebrate the undead. j.w even sent me a link on Facebook to a stranger’s blog post on Halloween and asked for my thoughts, which I responded by saying:

My thoughts from 2010: http://her.happyhippos.ca/2010/11/halloween/

Also, if the kids don’t go trick or treating (unless you go with them, because it would otherwise defeat the get-to-know-your-neighbours motive), it would be nice to have them participate in handing out candy. At our church, we had “harvest” nights, but not necessarily in lieu of trick or treating, as I think it started after dinner; kids could have probably gone to both. These Halloween alternative events were actually quite fun, as there were costume contests and carnival games with tickets to claim prizes at the end of the night (I hated the balloon shaving game though)! Although I didn’t invite any of my friends out (friends?, what friends?), I’m sure other kids did.
Further to my comment, I think the Halloween alternatives in my day were actually a safer place for young children especially, as these costume themes were almost always family friendly; e.g., animals in Noah’s ark. The children weren’t subjected to seeing dead corpses, gory zombies, or other spooky or grotesque characters, though fake, that are meant to scare.  I remember that as a child, I was suspectible to suggestion (or maybe just had a very active imagination); even the snow walkers in Star Wars: the Empire Strikes Back gave me nightmares (I saw these scenes after my supposed bedtime – I was awake and wandered out to the hallway for whatever reason). It also provided a great excuse and venue to dress up in costume, because what other day were you able to do that? (and no, I didn’t know about comic con and other themed cons back then) I also see these events as further protection from the dangers of lighting fireworks on Halloween – while in costume especially! Who knows how many burn or injury stories I’ve heard related to Halloween fireworks – why fireworks is even legal on Halloween is beyond me. Happened across these verses from Ephesians 5:11-16 (ESV) the night before Halloween, which seemed very Halloween-appropriate:
Take no part in the unfruitful works of darkness, but instead expose them. For it is shameful even to speak of the things that they do in secret. But when anything is exposed by the light, it becomes visible, for anything that becomes visible is light. Therefore it says, “Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead, and Christ will shine on you.” Look carefully then how you walk, not as unwise but as wise, making the best use of the time, because the days are evil.
— it even has a zombie-like feel to it when it says, “Awake, O sleeper, and arise from the dead…“! Anyway, while Halloween is probably the one day where it’s explicitly okay for most of our culture to dress up, and church goers frown upon this day, I notice that the above passage mentions days in plural, not just limited to one day like Halloween. The question is, as Christians, what is our motive when we take part or choose not to participate in such activities? And if it is to set ourselves apart to be in the world but not of the world, do our children understand why? (I felt exclusion and persecution as a child instead of understanding – it was only when I was a bit older that I could consciously voice why I was not actively participating or only taking part in selective activities).]]>
